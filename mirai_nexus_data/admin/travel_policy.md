# Travel Policy (Mirai Nexus) – Internal

This document outlines the standard travel procedures for employees of Mirai Nexus. All travel must adhere to these guidelines to ensure safety, cost-effectiveness, and compliance with company policies.

## 1. Air Travel
- **Domestic Flights:** Book economy class whenever possible.  
- **International Flights:** Premium economy is allowed for flights longer than 8 hours; business class requires executive approval.  
- **Booking:** All flights should be booked through the company’s approved travel portal.  
- **Changes/Cancellations:** Notify Travel Desk immediately. Any fees incurred for personal changes are the responsibility of the employee.

## 2. Accommodation
- **Hotels:** Use corporate-negotiated rates whenever possible. Standard single rooms are preferred.  
- **Upgrades:** Room upgrades for personal preference are not reimbursable unless pre-approved.  
- **Safety:** Choose hotels that meet minimum safety and hygiene standards.  

## 3. Travel Approval
- **Manager Approval:** Required for all trips.  
- **Department Head Sign-Off:** Required for trips longer than 5 consecutive days or trips involving international travel.  
- **Special Exceptions:** Executive-level travel may follow separate approval workflows.

## 4. Expenses and Reimbursements
- **Receipts:** Submit itemized receipts within 14 days of completing travel.  
- **Per-Diem:** Domestic travel per-diem capped at $60/day. International per-diem rates vary by country and must be approved in advance.  
- **Non-Reimbursable Expenses:** Personal entertainment, minibar charges, alcohol (unless business-related), and travel upgrades not pre-approved.  
- **Expense Reports:** All reports must be submitted via the company expense portal and include approvals from the manager.

## 5. Transportation
- **Ground Transport:** Use public transport or company-approved rideshare/taxi services whenever feasible.  
- **Rental Cars:** Must be approved in advance; standard-size vehicles preferred. Fuel costs and insurance are reimbursable per company policy.

## 6. Travel Safety & Conduct
- **Compliance:** Employees must comply with local laws and company conduct policies.  
- **Emergencies:** Report any incidents immediately to HR and the Travel Desk.  
- **Health & Safety:** Follow CDC/WHO guidance for international travel and maintain personal health insurance coverage.

## 7. Policy Review
- This policy is reviewed annually by Finance and HR departments to ensure alignment with company goals and industry best practices.
